THE VINEYARD

Our founding value is the respect for the environment and all forms of life so that our vines reach their balance to produce great grapes. Domaine Aurore CASANOVA, respectful of the environment and all life forms, was created in 2013 by Aurore and Jean-Baptiste.

It rests on singular terroirs: Puisieulx, Grand Cru of the Montagne de Reims: mainly Pinot Noirs (massal selection or clones) and Chardonnays growing on silts resting on hard limestone.

Le Mesnil-sur-Oger and Oger, two Grands Crus of the Côte des Blancs with 22 parcels and almost as many exposures! A common point, however: Chardonnay on a chalky subsoil with fine brown earth or chalky colluvium on the surface.

Champvoisy and Vauciennes in the Marne Valley: brown marl soil on hard limestone that allows Meunier to express itself.

Epernay, the capital of Champagne, and Chardonnay from the southern slopes.
This plurality of terroirs and grape varieties is enhanced by the careful observation and contemplation that guide Aurore and JB both in the vineyard and the cellar.

AURORE & JEAN-BAPTISTE

I am Aurore CASANOVA, daughter of a winemaker and a computer scientist. I grew up in Paris where I followed a sport-study program to become a professional classical dancer for 7 years abroad.

I am Jean-Baptiste ROBINET, from a winemaking family. I grew up in Champagne and worked in the paramedical field, designing a carbon prosthetic foot for amputees.

We met in 2011 during our viticulture and oenology training in Avize, Champagne. During his 2-year apprenticeship with Fabrice Pouillon, JB saw indigenous fermentations in barrels for the first time, observed soil work, and used his first plows.
